📚 Key Vocabulary

Could you tell me how to get to...?
Polite way to ask for directions
"Could you tell me how to get to the train station?"
Turn left / right at...
Basic direction instruction
"Turn left at the traffic light, then go straight."
It's on the corner of...
Describing a location at an intersection
"The hotel is on the corner of 5th and Main."
Am I on the right track?
Checking if you're heading in the right direction
"I'm looking for Central Park. Am I on the right track?"
How far is it?
Asking about distance
"How far is it? — About a ten-minute walk."
Which platform?
Asking about train/subway platform
"Which platform for the downtown train?"

🌍 Cultural Notes

In English-speaking countries, people often give directions using landmarks ('turn left at the Starbucks') rather than street names.
'I'm lost' is perfectly acceptable to say — don't be embarrassed.
On buses/trains, 'Is this the right stop for...?' is safer than trying to read every announcement.
Distances: 'It's a couple of blocks' means a short walk (2-3 minutes). 'It's a bit of a hike' means it's far.
