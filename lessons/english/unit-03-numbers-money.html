📚 Key Vocabulary

How much is this?
Asking the price of an item
"How much is this bag? — It's forty-five dollars."
That's a bit pricey
Polite way to say something is expensive
"Seventy dollars? That's a bit pricey for a T-shirt."
Can you do forty?
Casual negotiation, asking for a specific price
"Can you do forty? — I can do forty-five, that's my best."
I'll take it
Deciding to buy something
"I'll take it. — Great, cash or card?"
Keep the change
Telling the seller to keep the extra money as a tip
"Here's twenty. Keep the change."
Receipt, please
Asking for the purchase receipt
"Could I get a receipt, please?"

🌍 Cultural Notes

In English, prices are read with 'dollars and cents': $12.50 = 'twelve fifty' or 'twelve dollars and fifty cents'.
'Can I get a discount?' is normal at markets but weird at chain stores.
Tipping culture: 15-20% is standard at restaurants in the US; no tipping in the UK or Australia.
'Cash or card?' is the universal payment question.
