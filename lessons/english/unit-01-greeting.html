📚 Key Vocabulary

How's it going?
Casual greeting, similar to 'How are you?' but more informal
"Hey, how's it going? — Not bad, thanks!"
Nice to meet you
Polite phrase used when meeting someone for the first time
"Hi, I'm Sarah. — Nice to meet you, I'm James."
Catch you later
Informal way to say goodbye
"I gotta run. Catch you later! — Sounds good, see ya!"
What do you do?
Common way to ask about someone's job
"What do you do? — I work in marketing."
By the way
Used to introduce a new topic or add information
"By the way, have you met our new manager?"

🌍 Cultural Notes

In English-speaking workplaces, a firm handshake and eye contact are expected during first introductions.
'How are you?' is often a greeting, not a real question. 'Good, you?' is the standard autopilot response.
First names are common even in formal settings — don't assume you need to use titles unless the other person does.
