Introducing Yourself

📖 You're at a networking event. You need to introduce yourself clearly, talk about your background, and exchange contact info.

📝 Dialogue

Hi, I don't think we've met. I'm Michael.
你好，我们好像没见过。我是 Michael。
Opening introduction
B
Hi Michael, I'm Lisa. What brings you here today?
你好 Michael，我是 Lisa。你今天怎么过来的？

I'm a product manager at TechFlow. I'm interested in the AI talks.
我是 TechFlow 的产品经理。我对 AI 相关的演讲感兴趣。

📚 Key Vocabulary

I'm originally from...
Telling someone where you're from originally, not where you live now
"I'm originally from Chengdu, but I live in Shenzhen now."
What brings you here?
Polite way to ask why someone is at an event
"What brings you here today? — I'm interested in the AI track."
Let me give you my card
Offering your contact information
"Here, let me give you my card. Feel free to reach out anytime."
I work in/at...
Standard way to describe where you work
"I work at a startup called ByteCraft."
It was nice talking to you
Polite closing phrase
"Well, I should go. It was nice talking to you!"

🌍 Cultural Notes

The 'elevator pitch' — a 30-second self-introduction — is a prized skill in English-speaking business culture.
It's normal to share your role and company, but avoid oversharing personal details in a first meeting.
Business cards are less common now; exchanging LinkedIn profiles is the modern equivalent.
